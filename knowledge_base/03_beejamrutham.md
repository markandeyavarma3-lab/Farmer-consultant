# Beejamrutham (Bijamrita) — Seed and Seedling Treatment

> Recipe ratios vary across NITI Aayog, APCNF and Palekar sources. The proportions below are
> the commonly cited ones; confirm exact local ratios with a CRP before treating large seed lots.

## What it is and why it works

Beejamrutham is a treatment applied to seeds (or seedling roots) **before sowing**. It coats
the seed with beneficial micro-organisms and protective compounds that defend the young seed
and emerging root from soil-borne and seed-borne fungal and bacterial diseases during the
most vulnerable early stage of germination. It is the natural-farming equivalent of chemical
seed treatment, but chemical-free.

## Ingredients (to treat seed for roughly 1 acre)

| Ingredient            | Quantity      | Role                          |
|-----------------------|---------------|-------------------------------|
| Water                 | ~20 litres    | Medium                        |
| Cow dung (desi)       | ~5 kg         | Microbial protection          |
| Cow urine (desi)      | ~5 litres     | Antimicrobial + microbes      |
| Lime (chuna)          | ~50 g         | Anti-fungal, pH buffer        |
| Bund soil             | 1 handful     | Native microbe starter        |

## Preparation steps

1. Tie the ~5 kg cow dung in a cloth and suspend it in ~20 L water overnight.
2. Separately dissolve ~50 g lime in ~1 L water and leave overnight.
3. In the morning, squeeze the dung cloth into the water three times.
4. Add the ~5 L cow urine and the handful of bund soil.
5. Add the lime water and stir well.

## How to apply

1. Spread the seeds on a clean floor or tarpaulin.
2. Sprinkle/coat the seeds with Beejamrutham and mix gently by hand so every seed is coated.
3. Dry the seeds in shade (not direct sun).
4. Sow the same day, ideally within a few hours.

For seedlings/saplings, dip the roots in Beejamrutham before transplanting.

## Notes

- Use a clean container; avoid metal that may react.
- Do not over-soak seeds — a light, even coating is enough.
- This step pairs naturally with Pre-Monsoon Dry Sowing (see seasonal calendar).
