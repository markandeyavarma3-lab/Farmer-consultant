# Natural Pest Management — Neemastra, Brahmastra, Agniastra

> These are botanical/cow-based sprays used instead of chemical pesticides. Quantities below
> are commonly cited figures (per ~1 acre, diluted into ~200 L water for spraying). Pest
> identification matters — if the farmer cannot identify the pest, escalate to a CRP or KVK
> rather than guessing.

## Principle first: prevention over spraying

In natural farming, healthy diverse fields with 365-day green cover, mulch, and Jeevamrutham
rarely have severe pest outbreaks, because predator insects and balanced soil keep pests in
check. Sprays are a *last resort*, not a routine. Always start with: Is the field diverse?
Is there mulch? Are border/trap crops present? Are natural predators being preserved?

## Neemastra — for sucking pests (aphids, jassids, mealybugs, small insects)

The mildest of the three. Use first for light sucking-pest pressure.

| Ingredient        | Quantity     |
|-------------------|--------------|
| Water             | 200 litres   |
| Cow urine         | 5-10 litres  |
| Cow dung          | 2 kg         |
| Neem leaf/pulp    | 5-10 kg (crushed) |

Mix all, ferment **48 hours** in shade (stir twice daily), filter, and spray undiluted on the
crop. Storable for up to ~6 months.

## Brahmastra — for larger sucking pests and caterpillars / pod-borers

Stronger. Use when Neemastra is not enough or for caterpillars.

- Boil ~10 L cow urine with crushed pastes of pest-repellent leaves: neem, custard apple,
  guava, papaya, pomegranate, white datura, karanj (use what is locally available).
- Boil until reduced, cool, ferment ~24-48 hours, and filter.
- **Dilution:** roughly 6-8 litres of Brahmastra in 200 L water per acre for spraying.

## Agniastra — for leaf rollers, stem/fruit/pod borers, heavy caterpillar attack

The strongest; "agni" = fire, named for the chilli/garlic heat.

| Ingredient          | Quantity   |
|---------------------|------------|
| Cow urine           | 20 litres  |
| Neem leaf pulp      | 2 kg       |
| Tobacco             | 500 g      |
| Green chilli paste  | 500 g      |
| Garlic paste        | 250 g      |

Boil the mixture, let it ferment ~48 hours, filter. **Dilution:** roughly 6-8 litres in
200 L water per acre.

## Safety and good practice

- Spray in the cool of early morning or evening, never in peak heat.
- Always filter well to avoid clogging the sprayer.
- Wear cloth over nose/mouth; chilli/tobacco sprays irritate eyes and skin.
- Preserve beneficial insects — do not blanket-spray a whole field for a small localised attack.
- If a pest is unfamiliar or the outbreak is severe and spreading, this assistant should NOT
  guess. Escalate to a CRP or the nearest KVK for identification.

## Decision guide (which astra?)

1. Light sucking pests (aphids/jassids) -> **Neemastra** first.
2. Caterpillars / larger sucking pests, Neemastra insufficient -> **Brahmastra**.
3. Borers (stem/fruit/pod), heavy caterpillar attack -> **Agniastra**.
4. Unknown pest, or rapid spread -> **escalate to human expert**, do not guess.
