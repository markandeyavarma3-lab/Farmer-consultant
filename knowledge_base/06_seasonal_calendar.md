# Seasonal Calendar, Pre-Monsoon Dry Sowing & Crop Timing

> Timing guidance for Andhra Pradesh / south-Indian conditions. Exact dates depend on local
> rainfall and should be confirmed with a local CRP. Months are indicative.

## The two main seasons

- **Kharif (monsoon crop):** sown June-July with the south-west monsoon, harvested
  September-October. Main crops: paddy, cotton, maize, pulses, groundnut, millets.
- **Rabi (winter crop):** sown October-November on residual/stored moisture or irrigation,
  harvested February-March. Main crops: paddy (second crop), pulses (Bengal gram, black gram),
  groundnut, vegetables.
- **Zaid (summer):** short irrigated crops between March and May where water allows.

## Pre-Monsoon Dry Sowing (PMDS) — a key APCNF innovation

PMDS means sowing a **diverse mix of seeds into dry soil a few weeks BEFORE the monsoon
arrives** (typically April-May in AP). When the first rains come, the seeds germinate
immediately, so the field is green and protected from the very first drop of rain instead of
weeks later. Benefits:

- Extends the growing season — rainfed farmers can take an extra crop, sometimes up to three
  crops a year.
- Establishes 365-day green cover early.
- Builds soil cover before heavy rain, reducing erosion.
- The diverse mix (cereals + legumes + oilseeds + green manures) feeds the soil and spreads risk.

A typical PMDS seed mix has 15-30 species broadcast together. The legumes fix nitrogen, the
tall cereals give structure, the spreading crops cover ground, and green-manure species are
later cut and laid down as mulch.

## A simple year-round rhythm (natural farming)

| Period         | Activity                                                            |
|----------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|
| Mar-May        | Pre-Monsoon Dry Sowing of diverse mix; prepare Ghanajeevamrutham    |
| Jun-Jul        | Kharif sowing after Beejamrutham seed treatment; begin Jeevamrutham |
| Jul-Sep        | Apply Jeevamrutham every 7-10 days; mulch; monitor pests            |
| Sep-Oct        | Kharif harvest; lay residue as mulch; do not burn or bare the soil  |
| Oct-Nov        | Rabi sowing on stored moisture; Beejamrutham again                  |
| Dec-Feb        | Rabi crop care; Jeevamrutham; pest watch                            |
| Feb-Mar        | Rabi harvest; plan green cover for summer so soil is never bare     |

## Key decision moments where farmers most need advice

1. **Before sowing** — which crops/mix, seed treatment (Beejamrutham), PMDS timing.
2. **Early growth** — Jeevamrutham schedule, mulching.
3. **Pest appearance** — identify, decide if a spray is even needed, which astra.
4. **Between seasons** — keeping green cover so soil is never left bare.

The assistant should always ask the **crop**, the **growth stage**, and the **month/season**
before giving timing-specific advice, because the right answer changes completely with season.
