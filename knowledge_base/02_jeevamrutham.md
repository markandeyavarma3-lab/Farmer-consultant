# Jeevamrutham (Jeevamrutha) — Microbial Soil Inoculant

> Canonical figures based on NITI Aayog / APCNF descriptions. Quantities vary slightly
> between sources; the ranges below reflect that. When in doubt, use the lower quantity and
> consult a CRP.

## What it is and why it works

Jeevamrutham is a fermented liquid culture that multiplies beneficial soil micro-organisms
(bacteria and fungi). It is **not a fertiliser** — it does not directly feed the plant.
Instead it "inoculates" the soil so that native micro-organisms unlock the nutrients already
present in the soil and make them available to the crop. A handful of fertile soil from the
farm bund is added precisely to introduce these native microbes as a starter culture.

## Ingredients (for ~1 acre, ~200 litres)

| Ingredient        | Quantity        | Role                                  |
|-------------------|-----------------|---------------------------------------|
| Water             | 200 litres      | Medium                                |
| Cow dung (desi)   | 10 kg           | Microbial inoculant base              |
| Cow urine (desi)  | 5-10 litres     | Nitrogen + microbial food             |
| Jaggery           | 1-2 kg          | Energy source for microbes            |
| Pulse/gram flour  | 1-2 kg          | Protein source for microbes           |
| Bund/forest soil  | 1 handful       | Native microbe starter culture        |

Desi (indigenous breed) cow dung and urine are strongly preferred because they carry a
richer and more suitable microbial population than crossbred cattle.

## Preparation steps

1. Add the 10 kg cow dung and 5-10 L cow urine to a drum.
2. Add 1-2 kg jaggery and 1-2 kg pulse flour.
3. Add a handful of fertile farm-bund soil.
4. Fill with water to ~200 litres and stir well, clockwise.
5. Cover with a jute/cloth cloth (not airtight) and keep in shade.
6. Ferment for **48 hours**, stirring twice a day (morning and evening), clockwise.
7. Use within **about 7 days** of being ready — it loses potency after that.

## How to apply

- **With irrigation water:** let Jeevamrutham flow into the field along the irrigation channel.
- **As a foliar spray:** filter well and spray a diluted form on the crop.
- **Frequency:** roughly every 7-10 days, or at key crop stages. Typical use is
  100-200 litres per acre per application.

## Common mistakes to avoid

- Using an airtight container (the microbes need some air).
- Leaving it in direct sun (heat kills the culture).
- Storing it too long (use within a week).
- Using dung/urine from sick or antibiotic-treated animals.

## Ghanajeevamrutham (solid form)

A dry, storable version useful when liquid cannot be made often:

- 100 kg cow dung
- 1-2 kg jaggery
- 1-2 kg pulse flour
- 1 handful bund soil
- moisten with cow urine, mix, shade-dry, then store.

Storable for **6-8 months**. Broadcast over the field before sowing or with the seed.
