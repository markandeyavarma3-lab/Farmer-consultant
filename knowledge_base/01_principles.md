# The Foundations of Natural Farming (APCNF / ZBNF)

> Source basis: NITI Aayog (naturalfarming.niti.gov.in), APCNF / Rythu Sadhikara Samstha
> (apcnf.in), and Subhash Palekar's Zero Budget Natural Farming framework. Figures and
> recipes should be treated as advisory; always confirm locally with a Community Resource
> Person (CRP) before large-scale application.

## What Natural Farming Is

Natural Farming (NF) is a chemical-free, low-cost approach to agriculture that works *with*
natural ecological processes instead of against them. It removes the need for purchased
synthetic fertilisers and pesticides, replacing them with on-farm inputs prepared from cow
dung, cow urine, and locally available plant material.

In Andhra Pradesh it is implemented at scale as **Andhra Pradesh Community-managed Natural
Farming (APCNF)**, run by the not-for-profit **Rythu Sadhikara Samstha (RySS)**. It is the
largest agroecology programme in the world, reaching over 1.7 million farmers, and is built
on a farmer-to-farmer model where experienced farmers (Community Resource Persons) guide
new ones through a 3-to-5-year transition.

## Honest Framing of Benefits (important)

Natural Farming's strongest, best-evidenced benefit is a **large reduction in the cost of
cultivation**, because purchased chemical inputs are eliminated. Studies (NITI Aayog,
ICAR-NAARM, CSTEP) consistently report:

- Sharply lower input costs (fertiliser is only ~10-20% of NF material cost vs 50-70% conventionally)
- 50-60% less water and electricity use in many cases
- Lower greenhouse-gas emissions per acre

Yield evidence is **mixed**: the most careful studies conclude NF "may not be yield enhancing
but helped improve farmers' income by reducing cost of cultivation." Therefore the assistant
should frame NF around **lower cost and lower risk**, NOT around guaranteed higher yields.
Do not promise yield miracles.

## The Four Pillars (Subhash Palekar's ZBNF)

1. **Jeevamrutha** — a fermented microbial culture (cow dung + cow urine + jaggery + pulse
   flour + soil + water) applied to soil to multiply beneficial micro-organisms. It is a
   *bio-stimulant / microbial inoculant*, not a fertiliser. See `02_jeevamrutham.md`.

2. **Beejamrutha** — a seed/seedling treatment that protects young roots and seeds from
   soil-borne and seed-borne fungal and bacterial disease. See `03_beejamrutham.md`.

3. **Acchadana (Mulching)** — covering the soil with crop residue, live cover crops, or
   organic matter. Mulch conserves moisture, moderates soil temperature, suppresses weeds,
   and feeds soil life as it decomposes. Three types: live mulch (cover crops), straw/dry
   mulch (crop residue), and soil mulch (light surface tillage to break capillaries).

4. **Whapasa (soil moisture + aeration)** — Palekar's idea that healthy soil should hold
   roughly 50% air and 50% water vapour in its cavities, so crops need far less irrigation.
   Mulching and high soil-organic-matter make Whapasa possible, reducing water demand.

## The Nine Universal Principles of APCNF

1. **365 days of green cover** — keep living roots in the soil all year. Bare soil is dead
   soil; living roots feed soil micro-organisms continuously.
2. **Minimal disturbance of soil** — avoid deep ploughing; protect soil structure and the
   fungal-bacterial networks within it.
3. **Diverse, multi-tier cropping** — grow many crops of different heights and root depths
   together (see the five-layer model in `05_five_layer_model.md`).
4. **Integration of trees** into the farming system for shade, biomass, and biodiversity.
5. **Use of bio-stimulants** (Jeevamrutham, Ghanajeevamrutham, Beejamrutham) made from
   cow dung and urine to kick-start soil biology.
6. **Pre-Monsoon Dry Sowing (PMDS)** — sowing a diverse seed mix into dry soil before the
   monsoon so seeds germinate with the first rains, extending the growing season. See
   `06_seasonal_calendar.md`.
7. **No synthetic chemicals** — no chemical fertiliser, pesticide, herbicide, or fungicide.
8. **Botanical extracts for pest management** — Neemastra, Brahmastra, Agniastra and similar
   plant/cow-based sprays instead of chemical pesticides. See `04_pest_management.md`.
9. **Animal integration** — local breed cattle (especially desi cows) are central, as their
   dung and urine are the base of all inputs. One cow can support roughly 30 acres.

## Who Can Help on the Ground

When a question is beyond the scope of this assistant, or needs a field visit, the farmer
should be directed to a human:

- **RySS / APCNF Community Resource Person (CRP)** in their village or mandal — the primary
  source of trusted, local, hands-on guidance.
- **Kisan Call Centre**, toll-free **1800-180-1551**, 6 AM to 10 PM, available in Telugu,
  Hindi and other local languages.
- The nearest **Krishi Vigyan Kendra (KVK)** — district-level agricultural science centre.
